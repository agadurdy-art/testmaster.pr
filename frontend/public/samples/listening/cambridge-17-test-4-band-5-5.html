What's working

  • • Part 1 · 8/10: You handled note completion under conversational pace well — floors, fridge, shirts, balcony, electrician, police, training, review all locked in.
  • • Q12, Q13 (MCQ): You picked the right answer when the keyword was repeated almost verbatim — that's a real strength.
  • • Q15, Q19, Q20 (matching): "Meetings", "vouchers", "co-operative environment" — when the strategy is named explicitly, you map it correctly.

Where you lost the most marks

  • • Part 4 · only 3/10. Academic monologue + paraphrase. You wrote good for Q32, but the script said "a healthy alternative". You wrote thick for Q39 — script was "cloudy appearance". drop for Q40 instead of litre: you heard the meaning, missed the unit.
  • • Synonym confusion (Q7): You wrote dirt. Script said "capturing up to 99% of all the dust". Same idea, wrong word — IELTS marks ONE WORD ONLY, exact match.
  • • MCQ distractors (Q11, Q14): You picked the option that was mentioned in the audio, not the option that answered the question. Q11 the speaker says "many managers think it's [low pay] and long hours" — but the answer is what they don't realise: lack of training.
  • • Matching A–H (Q26–28, Q30): You skipped detail. Cricket helmet was unpopular among spectators, not players. Football caused injuries via wet leather → neck damage when headed.

Your 2-week plan to Band 6.5

  1. Daily: 1 Part 4 lecture from Cambridge 16–18, transcript closed first attempt. Aim 6/10 → 7/10 → 8/10.
  2. Synonym log: every wrong note-completion answer, write the script word AND your wrong word side by side. Review weekly.
  3. MCQ rule: circle the question stem keyword first. The audio always plants 2 distractors that sound like answers but don't match the stem.
  4. Matching: read all options A–H before audio starts. Mark "definitely no" as you eliminate during listening.
  5. Spelling: drill Part 1 vocabulary lists (rooms, services, food, jobs). One letter wrong = 0 marks.
