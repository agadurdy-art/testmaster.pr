# Stage 2: Starters (A1) - Master Data Set

Based on *Fun for Starters 4th Edition* and a **PhD-level Mastery-Based Curriculum**.

### Architecture Guidelines
- **Total Content:** 12 Units, 4 Lessons each (48 Lessons total)
- **Lesson Flow:** 10 steps (Retrieval -> Vocabulary -> Micro Game -> Reading -> Grammar -> Grammar Game -> Listening -> Production -> Exit Ticket -> Scheduled Review)
- **Visuals:** Every vocabulary word MUST have an accompanying image or emoji
- **Language:** Strictly English only for all lesson components

---

## UNIT 1: Say Hello!
- **Grammar:** Subject Pronouns (He/She/They), "Who is...?", Alphabet names.
- **Lesson 1:** Greetings. Vocab: hello, hi, answer, listen. Context: Meeting the teacher on the first day.
- **Lesson 2:** Friends. Vocab: they, name, new, old. Context: Introducing two new students, Ben and Kim.
- **Lesson 3:** Alphabet. Vocab: alphabet, spell, letter, word. Context: A spelling game in the classroom.
- **Lesson 4:** Unit Review. Spiral check of all Unit 1 items with distractors from Stage 1.

## UNIT 2: Numbers & Colors
- **Grammar:** "How many are there?", "There are [Number] [Color] [Objects]."
- **Lesson 1:** Numbers 11-15. Vocab: eleven, twelve, thirteen, fourteen, fifteen. Context: Counting colorful balloons at a party.
- **Lesson 2:** Numbers 16-20. Vocab: sixteen, seventeen, eighteen, nineteen, twenty. Context: Counting books on a library shelf.
- **Lesson 3:** New Colors. Vocab: purple, grey, brown, white, black, rainbow. Context: Describing the colors of a classroom rainbow.
- **Lesson 4:** Unit Review. Counting colored objects and identifying higher numbers.

## UNIT 3: What's in Your Classroom?
- **Grammar:** Prepositions of Place (in, on, under, next to, behind).
- *Vocabulary:* desk, chair, board, computer, door, window, wall, floor, picture.
- **Lesson 1:** Objects. Vocab: desk, chair, board, computer. Context: A tour of the school computer room.
- **Lesson 2:** Positions 1. Vocab: in, on, under. Context: Finding a lost pencil in the classroom.
- **Lesson 3:** Positions 2. Vocab: behind, next to, wall, floor. Context: Describing where pictures are hanging in the room.
- **Lesson 4:** Unit Review. "Find the object" challenge using prepositions.

## UNIT 4: Body & Action
- **Grammar:** Modal "Can / Can't", "Can you...?", Action verbs.
- *Vocabulary:* head, shoulders, knees, toes, eyes, ears, mouth, nose, arm, leg, hand, foot.
- **Lesson 1:** Body Review. Vocab: shoulders, knees, toes, arm, leg. Context: A morning exercise routine.
- **Lesson 2:** Actions. Vocab: jump, run, sing, dance. Context: A talent show where kids show what they can do.
- **Lesson 3:** Abilities. Vocab: can, can't, swim, climb. Context: Describing a robot's functions.
- **Lesson 4:** Unit Review. Combining body parts and actions (e.g., "I can jump with my legs").

## UNIT 5: Animals Everywhere
- **Grammar:** Present Simple (it lives, it eats), "Has it got...?", Physical descriptions.
- *Vocabulary:* snake, zebra, monkey, hippo, giraffe, elephant, spider, frog, lizard.
- **Lesson 1:** Zoo Animals. Vocab: giraffe, hippo, zebra, snake. Context: A virtual trip to the zoo.
- **Lesson 2:** Small Animals. Vocab: spider, frog, lizard, tail. Context: Looking for tiny animals in a garden.
- **Lesson 3:** Descriptions. Vocab: long neck, big ears, scary, funny. Context: Guessing an animal based on physical clues.
- **Lesson 4:** Unit Review. Sorting animals by size and habitat.

## UNIT 6: My Family & Friends
- **Grammar:** Possessive 's (Ben's father), "Who are they?", Plural nouns.
- *Vocabulary:* cousin, son, daughter, uncle, aunt, parent, baby, children, man, woman.
- **Lesson 1:** Family Tree. Vocab: son, daughter, baby, parent. Context: Looking at a family photo album.
- **Lesson 2:** Relatives. Vocab: uncle, aunt, cousin. Context: Talking about visitors coming for dinner.
- **Lesson 3:** People. Vocab: man, woman, children, person. Context: Describing people at a park.
- **Lesson 4:** Unit Review. Identifying family members and using possessive 's.

## UNIT 7: Food I Like! (was incorrectly labeled Unit 4 in source)
- **Grammar:** "Do you like...?", "Yes, I do / No, I don't", Conjunction "and".
- *Vocabulary:* chicken, rice, beans, water, juice, milk, bread, egg, fish, fruit.
- **Lesson 1:** Lunch Time. Vocab: chicken, rice, beans, bread. Context: Choosing food in the school canteen.
- **Lesson 2:** Drinks. Vocab: water, juice, milk, hot, cold. Context: Ordering drinks at a cafe.
- **Lesson 3:** Preferences. Vocab: like, don't like, favorite, fruit. Context: A survey about favorite snacks.
- **Lesson 4:** Unit Review. Creating a healthy meal plan.

## UNIT 8: My House
- **Grammar:** "Is there a...?", "Are there any...?", "There is / There are".
- *Vocabulary:* kitchen, bathroom, bedroom, living room, garden, stairs, mirror, clock, phone.
- **Lesson 1:** Rooms. Vocab: kitchen, bathroom, bedroom, living room. Context: Describing the different parts of a house.
- **Lesson 2:** Furniture. Vocab: mirror, clock, phone, stairs. Context: Finding objects while cleaning a room.
- **Lesson 3:** Existence. Vocab: there is, there are, many, some. Context: Describing what is inside a dream house.
- **Lesson 4:** Unit Review. Matching furniture to the correct rooms.

## UNIT 9: What are we doing?
- **Grammar:** Present Continuous (am/is/are + verb-ing).
- *Vocabulary:* wearing, eating, drinking, playing, sleeping, drawing, watching TV.
- **Lesson 1:** Current Actions. Vocab: eating, drinking, playing, wearing. Context: Describing a busy scene in a playground.
- **Lesson 2:** At Home Actions. Vocab: sleeping, drawing, watching TV, listening to music. Context: A phone call asking family members what they are doing.
- **Lesson 3:** Grammar focus. Focus on -ing endings and subject-verb agreement. Context: A "Mime the action" game.
- **Lesson 4:** Unit Review. Describing a picture of people doing various activities.

## UNIT 10: Clothes
- **Grammar:** "He/She is wearing...", Adjective + Noun order (e.g., blue skirt).
- *Vocabulary:* shirt, skirt, dress, trousers, shoes, socks, jacket, hat, glasses, handbag.
- **Lesson 1:** Basic Clothes. Vocab: shirt, skirt, dress, trousers. Context: Getting dressed for school in the morning.
- **Lesson 2:** Accessories. Vocab: shoes, socks, jacket, hat. Context: Packing a bag for a cold trip.
- **Lesson 3:** Descriptions. Vocab: glasses, handbag, clean, dirty. Context: Identifying people at a party by their clothes.
- **Lesson 4:** Unit Review. Dressing an avatar with specific colors and items.

## UNIT 11: Play & Hobbies
- **Grammar:** Verbs "Play, Go, Do", Adverbs of frequency (always, never).
- *Vocabulary:* guitar, piano, basketball, football, tennis, swim, paint, photo, radio.
- **Lesson 1:** Sports. Vocab: basketball, football, tennis, game. Context: Choosing a sport to play at the weekend.
- **Lesson 2:** Arts & Music. Vocab: guitar, piano, paint, photo. Context: An after-school hobby club.
- **Lesson 3:** Frequency. Vocab: always, never, sometimes, radio. Context: Describing daily hobby routines.
- **Lesson 4:** Unit Review. Creating a weekly hobby schedule.

## UNIT 12: Review & Final Gate
- **Focus:** Cumulative review of Units 1-11.
- **Lesson 1:** Basics Review. Grammar: Greetings, Numbers 1-20, Colors. Context: A welcome back party.
- **Lesson 2:** Description Review. Grammar: Body, Clothes, Animals, Family. Context: Describing a "Mystery Person".
- **Lesson 3:** Usage Review. Grammar: Present Continuous, Can/Can't, Likes/Dislikes. Context: A weekend trip story.
- **Lesson 4:** Starters Mock Exam. Full Mastery Check. Context: "The Island Adventure" where students use all skills to find a treasure and earn their Stage 2 Certificate.
